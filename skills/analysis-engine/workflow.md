Analysis Workflow

Canonical workflow to apply for analytical tasks; each step produces auditable outputs.

1. Observe
  - Record raw observations with provenance.
2. Collect evidence
  - Gather direct and indirect evidence, score and annotate.
3. Generate hypotheses
  - Create explicit, testable hypotheses; enumerate alternatives.
4. Rank hypotheses
  - Use evidence aggregation and priors to rank hypotheses quantitatively.
5. Verify
  - Execute prioritized verification steps; prefer independent checks.
6. Reject invalid ones
  - Remove hypotheses contradicted by verified evidence; document eliminations.
7. Produce final answer
  - Present conclusion with evidence list, confidence, verification steps taken, and next recommended actions.

Notes
- Maintain at least two alternative hypotheses until sufficient evidence exists.
- Always include a verification plan when presenting a conclusion.
